I'm a quantitative researcher at Orbis Investments in London, where I build systematic investment signals and risk models. On the side I write data-driven economics at Parth's Datastack — using data to think out loud about how the economy actually works.

The two threads are really the same habit: take a messy question, point data at it, and see what holds up. Lately that's meant replicating the 2025 Nobel Prize in economics for a general audience, and digging into where elite talent actually flows — and what it costs the rest of us.

A few things I've made:

  • The Great Talent Misallocation — why so much talent pours into finance, and when that becomes everyone's problem.
  • Creative Destruction — the 2025 economics Nobel, explained.
  • Modelling Political Polarization — an interactive model from my maths dissertation.

Before Orbis I studied financial economics and mathematics at the University of St Andrews, where I founded STAISH — the university's first AI safety fellowship, a six-week programme for a cohort of 25. I've since passed CFA Level I (top 5% globally), and my dissertation, Modeling the Divide, is here (PDF).
