What I'm working on

By day I'm a quantitative researcher at Orbis Investments in London. I design systematic stock-selection signals from company fundamentals, build factor and risk models that separate luck from genuine skill in a portfolio, and increasingly use large language models to pull structured signal out of messy text like earnings-call transcripts. The throughline is turning statistical research into tools a portfolio manager can actually act on.

On the side I write Parth's Datastack, where I use data to think out loud about economics — recently replicating the 2025 Economics Nobel for a general audience, and digging into where talent really flows in the economy. See the writing →

Before all this, at the University of St Andrews, I founded STAISH — the university's first AI safety fellowship — a six-week program that took a cohort of 25 students through the technical and governance sides of AI risk.

Background

I studied Financial Economics and Mathematics at the University of St Andrews (2020–2024), and I've passed CFA Level I (top 5% globally). My mathematics dissertation, Modeling the Divide, built a 5,000-agent simulation of political polarization — showing how unequal exposure and starting conditions can pull a population toward the extremes. I've since rebuilt it as an interactive app, or read the original (PDF).
